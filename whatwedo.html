What We Do

There has never been a more exciting time for research into BRCA cancers: awareness is at an all-time high; testing is getting much less expensive; studies suggest more cancers than ever known may derive from BRCA or BRCA-like mutations; and new drugs are having dramatic results. BRCA Foundation investigators are on the vanguard of solving all these challenges.

The BRCA Foundation is currently funding research from Harvard Medicine BRCA1 Project, a collaborative endeavor partnering Harvard Medical School and Dana-Farber Cancer Institute; Stanford Cancer Institute; and the University of California, San Francisco, Center for BRCA Research. Mission

To accelerate research and foster collaboration in order to prevent
and cure BRCA cancers

The core mission for BRCA Foundation is to accelerate reseach and foster collaboration to prevent and cure BRCA cancers. We invest in the most promising work of top scientifics investigators while creating a forum for successful partnerships across institutions, all with the goal of getting the fastest possible results.
